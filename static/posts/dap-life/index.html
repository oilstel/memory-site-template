DAP = Digital Audo Player

Friday October 25, 2024 at 0:00

I just ordered a Sony NW-E394 Walkman (8GB), arriving on Saturday.

Exciting!

The other day, I was walking and watching the fall colors, wishing I had an easy way to listen to music while in motion.

A more practical motivation... my iPhone’s lightning connector is faulty, making it impossible to listen to music while walking. Even before that, I was always misplacing the headphone adapter. I also find it distracting to constantly look at my phone just to change a song.

Earlier this evening, I was at XP for Wietske's darning workshop. It was so nice to do a focused activity like that. Later, someone noticed that Kirsten had an iPod Nano on her table. It reminded me of my DAP thoughts from the other day and inspired me to take the plunge into DAP life...

I’m ready for DAP life. I plan to keep a diary of my findings here. Stay tuned...

13:56
A package arrives thanks to PostNL. DAP life begins. Unpacking happens on the big table.

First impressions

  • The DAP is lighter than I expected
  • There's a funny sticker of the actual interface stuck to the screen
  • It comes with preloaded songs and "sample photos" lol. These include Fall.mp3, It's Complicated.mp3, and Silence.mp3.
  • It's simple to add mp3s to the device. It shows up as a USB when plugged into my computer and I can just drag & drop mp3s into the "MUSIC" folder.
  • All the buttons feel intuitive, even more so than an iPod. Maybe it's just me but I never really loved the scroll wheel on the iPod.

I look at the instruction manual while talking on the phone. It's mostly printed in grays and blues. I like the drawings. They are kinda generic but playful. The character moves around the page with earbuds in. In one panel the battery runs low and a question mark appears above the character. It makes me want to ask some questions about DAP life...
